Infected infants often present with non-specific signs that are commonly seen with non infected conditions such as transient tachypnea of the newborn or TTN.repiratiry distress syndrome orRDS,cogential heart disease or inborn errors of metabolism.


Neonatal sepsis is rare, yet, the infant's presentation may be so similar to other illnesses, that clinicians often err on the side of caution because of the devastating effects of delayed treatment of infection.


Although reduction in the use of unnecessary antibiotics is a national and international goal, it is common to start antibiotics when neonates have clinical signs of sepsis, while awaiting laboratory testing and resolution of the clinical signs.


Reduction in use of unnecesssary antibiotics is a national and international goal.
